Detailed Operating Procedure for an AI Property Research Agent

1. Purpose
This SOP explains exactly how the AI Property Research Agent must collect client requirements, search the market, validate property information, benchmark each opportunity, identify risks, rank suitable properties, prepare a client-ready property summary and update Kommo. The procedure is written so a trained staff member can follow it manually and so the same logic can be configured into an AI workflow.
2. Scope
House and land packages
Newly completed homes
Turnkey packages
First home buyer opportunities
Owner-occupier properties
Investment properties
SMSF-suitable properties, subject to professional financial and legal advice
3. Core Operating Rules
Never recommend a property only because it is available. It must also fit the client, represent reasonable value and have its key risks disclosed.
Never state that a package is fixed price or turnkey unless all required inclusions and exclusions have been checked.
Never present an expired, unverified or unavailable package.
Never exceed the client’s confirmed maximum budget without clearly labelling the shortfall and obtaining consultant approval.
All government grants, finance outcomes, rental estimates and growth observations must be presented as subject to eligibility, professional advice and independent verification.
Every factual item must record its source and date checked.

Step 1: Open the Assignment and Create the Research Record
Objective: Create a complete audit trail before any searching begins.
Instructions
Open the client’s Kommo record and the assigned task. Confirm the client name, assigned consultant, due date and priority.
Create a new research entry using the naming format: Client Name – State/Area – Budget – Date.
Record the date and time the research starts, the AI Agent name/version and the sources that will be searched.
Check whether a previous search exists. Reuse relevant information only after rechecking availability and pricing.
Mark the task as “Research in Progress”.
Required output
A dated research record linked to the correct client
A clear task owner and deadline
A list of required sources
Quality check
Correct client record opened
No duplicate live task exists
Step 2: Confirm and Structure the Client Brief
Objective: Convert the client’s notes into clear mandatory requirements and flexible preferences.
Instructions
Extract the maximum purchase budget. Separate the approved borrowing limit from the client’s preferred spending limit.
Record deposit amount, finance pre-approval status, expected finance date and any known lender restrictions.
Classify the buyer as first home buyer, owner-occupier, investor or SMSF buyer.
Record preferred state, suburbs and acceptable nearby alternatives. Add maximum commuting distance or travel time where relevant.
Record required bedrooms, bathrooms, car spaces, storeys, minimum land size and minimum house size.
Record title preference, build timeframe and required settlement or move-in date.
Record lifestyle needs such as schools, public transport, shopping, employment access, medical services and community facilities.
For investors, record target rent, target yield, cash-flow preference, vacancy tolerance and growth objectives.
Classify every requirement as Mandatory, Preferred or Optional.
If a mandatory field is missing, create a clarification task for the assigned consultant. Continue preliminary research only if the missing information will not materially change the search.
Required output
Completed client brief
Mandatory/preferred/optional requirement list
Search radius and budget limits
Quality check
Budget and buyer type confirmed
No contradiction between CRM notes and finance information
Escalate when
Budget is unclear
Client requirements conflict
Finance status is older than 30 days or marked uncertain
Step 3: Build the Search Strategy
Objective: Decide where and how the AI will search before opening listings.
Instructions
Create search filters from the client brief: state, suburb, price ceiling, property type, bedrooms, land size and title status.
List primary suburbs first, then nearby suburbs that meet the client’s travel and lifestyle needs.
Set the source order: internal stock and approved builders, Proxima, E-Agent, developer portals, builder websites, realestate.com.au and Domain.
Define a comparison radius. Use the same suburb first, then comparable nearby suburbs where stock is limited.
Define the minimum number of properties to review. Review at least 10 to 20 options per client where market supply permits.
Define rejection rules before searching, including price above budget, wrong property type, unacceptable title timeframe, missing mandatory rooms or unacceptable risk.
Required output
Search filters
Source sequence
Comparison area
Predefined rejection rules
Step 4: Search and Capture Candidate Properties
Objective: Collect a broad but relevant pool of available properties.
Instructions
Search each approved source using the agreed filters.
Open the full listing or package document, not only the search-result headline.
Capture the property address or lot number, estate, suburb, state and postcode.
Capture builder, developer, land size, house size, house design, bedrooms, bathrooms, car spaces and storey type.
Capture land price, build price, package price, deposit requirements, promotions and expiry dates.
Capture title status, expected title date, construction timeframe and estimated completion date.
Capture all advertised inclusions and exclusions.
Copy the source link or document name and record the date checked.
Remove exact duplicates. Keep alternative designs on the same lot only when they offer a meaningful price or layout difference.
Apply the initial rejection rules and record the reason for every rejected candidate.
Required output
Candidate property register
Source and date for every candidate
Rejection log
Quality check
At least 10 candidates reviewed where available
No duplicate package counted as a separate market option
Step 5: Verify Availability and Current Pricing
Objective: Ensure the package still exists and the information is current.
Instructions
Check the builder or developer portal for live availability.
Where live status is unclear, flag the package for human confirmation by email or phone before it is client-ready.
Confirm land price, build price, total package price and any promotion expiry.
Confirm the house design can be built on the nominated lot.
Confirm title status and expected title date.
Confirm deposit requirements and contract structure, including whether land and build contracts are separate.
Record “Verified”, “Pending Confirmation” or “Unavailable”.
Do not place a property in the final recommendation list while its status is Pending Confirmation unless the report clearly states this and a consultant approves.
Required output
Verification status
Verification date
Current confirmed price
Quality check
No unavailable property remains shortlisted
Any pending verification is visibly labelled
Escalate when
Builder information conflicts with portal information
Price has changed materially
Title date is uncertain
Step 6: Validate the Full Package Price and Turnkey Status
Objective: Calculate the realistic total cost and identify missing inclusions.
Instructions
Break the advertised package price into land price, build price and stated allowances.
Check whether site costs are fixed, allowed for, capped or subject to soil test and engineering.
Check developer design requirements and mandatory facade, material or landscaping obligations.
Check driveway, fencing, front and rear landscaping, flooring, blinds, heating, cooling, stone benchtops, appliances, lighting, clothesline, letterbox, NBN/communications and council requirements.
Check whether demolition, retaining walls, rock removal, service connections, bushfire requirements or acoustic treatments may add cost.
Create an “Estimated Additional Costs” line for every missing or uncertain item.
Classify the package as Full Turnkey, Partial Turnkey, Base Package or Unclear.
Use the realistic total cost, not only the headline price, when checking the client budget.
Required output
Price breakdown
Turnkey classification
List of exclusions and estimated additional costs
Quality check
No package described as turnkey without evidence
Budget test uses realistic total cost
Step 7: Benchmark Land and Package Pricing
Objective: Determine whether the property is competitively priced.
Instructions
Search vacant land in the same suburb and estate on realestate.com.au, Domain and developer listings.
Compare land size, price, title status, orientation, frontage, corner position and estate stage.
Calculate approximate land price per square metre when useful. Treat this as a comparison indicator, not a valuation.
Search comparable new house and land packages with similar bedrooms, house size, land size and turnkey inclusions.
Compare the total realistic price, not just the advertised headline.
Identify the three strongest comparable options where possible.
Classify the candidate as Below Market, Competitive, Slightly Above Market or Poor Value and provide the reason.
Where the candidate is more expensive, explain whether the difference is justified by larger land, larger house, better inclusions, title status or location.
Required output
Land comparison table
Package comparison table
Value classification with explanation
Quality check
At least three comparables where market supply permits
Comparables are genuinely similar
Step 8: Assess Builder and Product Quality
Objective: Identify construction, reputation and delivery risks.
Instructions
Check the approved builder database for licence status, service areas, product range, warranty information and known issues.
Review recent publicly available customer feedback and internal SPB experience. Do not rely on a single review.
Check whether the builder regularly delivers the advertised product type and build area.
Compare standard inclusions, site-cost policy, variation process, estimated build time and communication quality.
Identify whether the builder has unresolved internal concerns or requires management approval.
Rate Builder Confidence as High, Medium or Low and give the reason.
Escalate legal, insolvency, licence or major defect concerns to management. The AI must not make unsupported allegations.
Required output
Builder assessment
Builder confidence rating
Known limitations or approval requirement
Step 9: Assess Location, Demand and Amenity
Objective: Explain whether the location supports the client’s lifestyle or investment goals.
Instructions
Confirm travel distance or time to the client’s nominated employment, family or preferred area.
Identify nearby schools, shopping centres, public transport, hospitals, parks and major roads.
For investors, review current rental listings for comparable properties and record an estimated rent range.
Review vacancy, population, infrastructure and supply indicators from reliable sources where available.
Identify planned infrastructure carefully and state whether it is proposed, funded, under construction or completed.
Assess whether the estate and suburb are predominantly owner-occupier, investor-focused or mixed where evidence supports this.
Summarise the location in plain English, including both strengths and limitations.
Required output
Amenity summary
Estimated rent range
Location strengths and limitations
Quality check
No unverified infrastructure claim stated as certain
Rental estimate based on comparable current or recent listings
Step 10: Complete Property Risk Checks
Objective: Identify risks that could affect cost, financeability, liveability, rental demand or resale.
Instructions
Check flood mapping, bushfire-prone land, planning overlays and known environmental constraints using official or reliable sources.
Check the lot plan and available documents for easements, unusual shape, narrow frontage, slope, retaining requirements and orientation.
Check title timing, sunset dates or extended delays where relevant.
Check body corporate or owners corporation fees for townhouses and units.
Check proximity to major roads, rail, industrial land, powerlines or other possible amenity concerns.
Check for oversupply risk, high investor concentration or weak rental evidence where data is available.
Rate each identified risk as Low, Medium or High. Explain the basis and proposed mitigation.
Escalate any High risk before recommending the property.
Required output
Risk register
Overall risk rating
Mitigation or further checks required
Quality check
Every property has a documented risk assessment
High risks are escalated
Step 11: Score and Rank the Properties
Objective: Apply consistent rules to determine the strongest options.
Instructions
Score Budget Fit out of 20 based on the realistic total price and finance position.
Score Client Requirement Match out of 20 based on mandatory and preferred features.
Score Value and Competitiveness out of 15 using the benchmark results.
Score Location and Amenity out of 15.
Score Builder Confidence and Product Quality out of 10.
Score Rental/Investment Suitability out of 10 for investors, or Lifestyle Suitability for owner-occupiers.
Score Risk out of 10, with lower-risk properties receiving higher points.
Calculate the total score out of 100 and add a written explanation.
Rank candidates from highest to lowest. Do not allow the numerical score to override a failed mandatory requirement.
Select three to five final options. If fewer than three qualify, state the market limitation and show the best realistic alternatives.
Required output
100-point score for each shortlisted property
Ranked shortlist
Reason for selection or rejection
Quality check
No failed mandatory requirement hidden by a high score
Scores are supported by evidence
Step 12: Prepare the Property Summary
Objective: Create a clear one-page or concise client-ready summary for every recommended property.
Instructions
Use the Property Summary Template in Section 7 of this SOP.
Write a two-to-four sentence executive summary explaining why the property suits the client.
Present confirmed property facts in a simple table.
Show the full price breakdown, inclusions, exclusions and possible additional costs.
Include location highlights, rental estimate where relevant, value comparison and risk rating.
Include the AI suitability score and recommendation: Recommend, Recommend with Conditions, Hold or Do Not Recommend.
Use plain English. Avoid guarantees about growth, rent, finance approval, grants or completion timing.
Add “Information checked on [date] and subject to availability and independent verification.”
Required output
Completed property summary for each recommended option
Clear recommendation and conditions
Client disclaimer
Step 13: Prepare and Clean Supporting Brochures
Objective: Provide accurate client-facing material without confidential supplier information.
Instructions
Create a working copy of the original brochure.
Remove builder and supplier contact details only where SPB is authorised to do so and the material can legally be modified.
Remove internal commissions, referral fees, wholesale pricing and confidential notes.
Do not remove required legal disclaimers or change material property facts.
Add approved SPB branding and contact details.
Check the suburb, state, lot number, price, room count, land size and house size against the verified research record.
Label artist impressions, indicative images and non-standard upgrades accurately.
Save the file using: Suburb – Property Type – Price – Date – Client Copy.
Required output
Clean client brochure
Consistent file name
No confidential information
Quality check
Brochure facts match research record
Required disclaimers remain visible
Step 14: Update Kommo and Send for Consultant Review
Objective: Create a complete record and obtain human approval before client presentation.
Instructions
Upload the research register, property summaries, comparison tables and cleaned brochures to the correct Kommo record.
For every shortlisted property, record price, verification date, availability status, score, risks and recommendation.
Record rejected properties and rejection reasons to avoid repeated work.
Create a consultant review task with a due date and recommended next action.
Send a concise internal note listing the best option, alternatives, compromises and any items awaiting confirmation.
Change the task status to “Awaiting Consultant Review”.
Do not send the package directly to the client unless the AI workflow has explicit approval and all required checks are complete.
Required output
Updated Kommo record
Consultant review task
Complete audit trail
Quality check
Correct client record and files
All dates, statuses and risks recorded
Step 15: Final Quality Assurance and Close the Task
Objective: Ensure the output is accurate, complete and usable.
Instructions
Run the final checklist in Section 8.
Confirm every shortlisted property meets all mandatory client requirements or clearly states the compromise.
Confirm all prices, availability dates, title information and inclusions are sourced and current.
Confirm all risks and uncertainties are visible in the summary.
Check spelling, grammar, formatting and SPB branding.
Confirm Kommo is updated and the consultant has received the review task.
Close the research task only after the consultant accepts the report or requests no further changes.
If a property becomes unavailable before presentation, reopen the research task and replace it.
Required output
QA-complete report
Closed or returned task with reason
Current shortlist

10. Exception Handling
Situation
Required response
No suitable property within budget
Search nearby suburbs, smaller land, smaller house, townhouses or later title dates. Record each compromise and ask the consultant whether finance capacity can be reassessed.
Only one or two suitable options
Provide the genuine options. Do not add weak properties to reach a numerical target. Explain the limited supply.
Price or availability cannot be verified
Mark Pending Confirmation and do not recommend as final until verified or consultant-approved.
High-risk overlay or site issue
Escalate for specialist or human review. Do not minimise the risk.
Conflicting information between sources
Use the builder/developer or contract information as primary where current, document the conflict and obtain confirmation.
Grant, finance or SMSF question
Refer to the finance, legal or tax professional. The AI may summarise general criteria but must not confirm eligibility or provide personal advice.
